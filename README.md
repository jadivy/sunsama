## What Even Is Sunsama?

Think of Sunsama as that friend who gently reminds you that you can't actually clone yourself. It's a daily planner that pulls together your calendar, emails, and tasks from a bunch of different apps into one clean workspace. The whole idea is to help you plan what you can *actually* get done in a day, not just pile on tasks until your brain melts.

The app was built by a small team that clearly got tired of productivity tools that make you feel bad about yourself. Instead of rewarding you for checking off the most boxes, Sunsama encourages you to set realistic goals and actually finish your day feeling calm. Wild concept, right?

## The Features That Actually Matter

**Daily Planning Ritual**: Every morning, you review your tasks, set priorities, and time-block your day. It's guided, so you're not just staring at a blank screen wondering where to start.

**Time Blocking**: Drag tasks onto your calendar. See exactly how much time you have versus how much work you've planned. Get warnings when reality and ambition don't match.

**Focus Mode**: Hides everything except what you're working on right now. Plus there's a Pomodoro timer if you're into that.

**Weekly Objectives**: Set your big goals for the week. Your daily tasks can link to these objectives, so you're not just churning through random work.

**Time Tracking**: Track how long tasks actually take versus how long you thought they'd take. Over time, you get better at estimating (in theory).

**Daily Shutdown**: At day's end, review what got done, reschedule what didn't, and actually close out your workday instead of letting it bleed into your evening.

**Channels and Contexts**: Organize work by categories. Super useful for balancing work versus personal tasks, or seeing where your time actually goes.

## The Honest Downsides

Let's be real for a second. Sunsama has some limitations that might be dealbreakers depending on what you need.

The mobile situation is rough. You basically need to use desktop or web for actual planning. The iOS and Android apps are fine for checking what's on your schedule, but you can't do the full planning flow. If you're someone who likes to organize your day while sitting on the subway, this won't work.

You can only plan about two weeks out. So if you're trying to map out a three-month project, you'll hit a wall. The app is really designed for daily and weekly planning, not long-term project management.

Updates roll out slowly. The team is small and deliberate, which means you're not getting new features every week. Some users get frustrated waiting for things they've requested.

No AI automation. If you want an app that will auto-schedule your tasks or suggest optimal times, look elsewhere. Sunsama is firmly in the "manual, intentional planning" camp.
